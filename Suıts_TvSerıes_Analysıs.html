Introduction¶

Title of the Show is a legal drama television series that premiered in 2011. The show follows talented college dropout Mike Ross, who starts working as a law associate for Harvey Specter, despite never having attended law school. The series has been well received for its sharp writing and strong performances.

Data Overview¶

The dataset contains viewership numbers sourced from Wikipedia for each season's premiere and finale, along with average viewership and demographic ratings

Observations and Analysis¶

Through the analysis of the viewership data, several trends become apparent:

The show's viewership peaked in Season 1 with an average of 4.28 million viewers. There has been a noticeable decline in viewership over the seasons, with the steepest drop occurring between Seasons 4 and 5.
